How Light Pollution Affects Nocturnal Ecosystems

In natural darkness, nocturnal animals have evolved sophisticated adaptations for hunting and survival. Predators use low-light vision and stealth, while prey rely on darkness for camouflage and escape.

When artificial light is introduced:

  • Prey become more visible to predators, increasing encounter rates
  • Predators' hunting efficiency may decrease due to glare and altered shadows
  • Natural behaviors are disrupted, leading to higher stress and energy expenditure
  • Population dynamics shift, potentially causing trophic cascades
